Explore Hamilton

There are many great places to visit in and around Hamilton, and here are some notable locations and venues that are a must-visit for your stay in this city!



Hamilton Gardens

Want a relaxing day out, surrounded by beautiful nature in this historical site? Gorgeous plants, rivers and lakes, a lovely place to explore with many different garden designs in this one site. Hamilton Gardens is a large public garden park that is perfect for a picnic day out to relax and unwind, and has tons of fun events for the family young and old.

Find it at Hungerford Crescent, or visit its website to learn more and see what events are happening.



Waikato Museum

Waikato Museum shows the best of Waikato and New Zealand. Its rich history, the stories of the time, stunning visual art, tangata whenua and even some science. This is a great place to visit to learn about the region, and provide some interesting entertainment that is both fun and highly educational.

Find it at 1 Grantham St, or visit its website to learn more and see when you can visit.



Hamilton Zoo

Hamilton Zoo is the perfect place to bring your family to explore the wonder of animals in this wildlife sanctuary. This destination is home to a wide variety of animals, with generous habitats. With native and and foreign flora and fauna. It also has many events to meet the animals face to face and learn more about them. Hamilton Zoo is also comitted to conservation of animals and the environment, so you know you're in a location that has a good cause.

Find it at 183 Brymer Rd, or visit its website to learn more about it and the lovely animals within.



Waitomo Caves

A little out of Hamilton, but close enough that you should definitely visit. A stunning work of nature, take a world-famous boat ride through this vast and extensive, naturally formed, underground limestone cave system. Where thousands of glow worms live and light up the caves in a gorgeous and luminescent hue and learn about the extensive culture and natural history of the area.

Find out more at Waitomo's website.
